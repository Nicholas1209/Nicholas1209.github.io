About Me

I'm excited to share a little bit about myself with you. I was born in Shenzhen, China, as the son of a German father and and a Chinese mother. 

 
At the age of 8, my mother and I moved to a small town in Germany, called Diez to reunite with my father.


I graduated from a German high school, in which I majored in English and Mathematics in 2018, after which I decided to pursue my post-secondary education in the United States, as I was always fascinated by American culture and the close-knit nature of American college campuses. 

 
Here, at California State University Monterey Bay, I am majoring in Business Administration with a concentration in Information Systems. So far, I've learned valuable skills in SQL, Python, and R, that I am eager to apply to real-life projects.
I will be graduating in May 2023 Magna Cum Laude. 

 
Five years from now, I see myself in a Marketing Research Analyst position.
My marketing professor, Heiko Wieland, defines marketing as “the process by which companies create value for customers but also for society at large.”
At CSUMB, where the responsible aspect of doing business is heavily emphasized through the Quintuple Bottom Line, I’ve learned that stockholder value is not the ultimate goal. I want to be responsible for products that achieve maximum utility for the customer and other relevant stakeholders and help transform the status quo of society into something more positive.
